Step 1 · Rehearse first

Practice the meeting before the meeting.

Got a presentation, a status update, or a tough discussion coming up? Tell your coach what it's about and practice out loud. It plays the room, asks the questions you might get, and helps you find the words while the stakes are low.

By the time it's real, you've already said it once, or five times.

Step 2 · Natural phrasing

Say it the way a native speaker would.

You know what you want to say. Your coach helps you say it the natural way, smoothing the phrasing that's technically correct but sounds stiff, and showing you the version people actually use at work.

This isn't about changing your accent or hiding where you're from. It's about being understood the first time, every time.

Phrasing check
Coach read · before your email goes out
"I am writing to inform you that the deadline cannot be respected by our team."
StiffCorrect, but formal and a little hard to read. Native speakers would say it more directly.
"Heads up: we're not going to make the deadline. Here's what we can do instead."
NaturalClear, friendly, and gets to the point. This is how it usually sounds at work.
Step 3 · In the moment

The hardest part is jumping in. Get the phrases ready.

In a fast meeting, the moment to speak passes quickly. Your coach gives you ready-made phrases for the situations that trip people up: interrupting politely, disagreeing without sounding harsh, buying a second to think, or asking someone to repeat themselves.

Keep them in your back pocket so you never lose your turn because you couldn't find the words fast enough.

Phrases for the moment
Coach read · keep these handy
  • To jump in
    "Can I add one thing to that?" / "Building on what Sara said..."
  • To disagree
    "I see it a little differently. Here's why..."
  • To buy time
    "That's a good question. Let me think for a second."
Asking to repeat is normal
"Sorry, could you say that again?" is something native speakers say all the time. It's not a weakness.

Fluency isn't the same as being heard.

You can be brilliant at your job and still watch a less-qualified colleague get credit for an idea you had first, simply because they said it more smoothly in the moment. That's not a gap in your ability. It's a gap in reps.

Work Coach gives you those reps. It's a patient, private place to practice, and a coach that shows you exactly what's working. This is about your accent being no obstacle to your ideas, not about hiding where you're from.

Frequently asked questions

Is this trying to remove my accent?

No. Your accent is part of who you are, and there's nothing to fix about it. Work Coach helps with clarity and confidence: finding natural phrasing, learning the vocabulary your role uses, and getting comfortable speaking up. The goal is for your ideas to land, accent and all.

Will my coworkers know Work Coach is there?

No. Work Coach captures audio locally on your Mac. There is no bot in the call and no notification, so nobody else in the meeting can tell it's there.

What do I need to get started?

Start your free trial and tell your coach about your role and where you want to feel more confident in English. It helps you rehearse, reviews your real meetings, and builds vocabulary from there. Every plan begins with a 2-week free trial, with no credit card required.

Is this only for beginners?

Not at all. Many people are fluent in English and still want to sound sharper in high-stakes moments, speak up faster in meetings, or master the idioms of a new industry. Your coach meets you at your level.

How does the rehearsal work?

Your coach plays the room, whether that's your manager, your team, or a client, and asks the questions you're likely to get. You practice out loud, get feedback on clarity and phrasing, and run it again as many times as you want.

Is my data private?

Yes. Your recordings, transcripts, and notes stay private to you. They're never shared with your employer or other users, and never used to train shared models. You can delete everything anytime.
