Step 1 · Benchmark

Know exactly where you stand against the next level.

"Work hard and it'll happen" is how people stay stuck. Your coach maps your current work against what the next level actually requires and shows you, concretely, where you're already operating there and where you're not.

No more guessing what "ready" means. You can see the exact distance left to close.

Step 2 · Close the gaps

Turn the gaps into a plan you can actually work.

A gap you can name still has to be closed. Your coach breaks each one into concrete moves: the project to volunteer for, the meeting to speak up in, the skill to demonstrate, on a timeline that lines up with your review cycle.

That way you've got real progress to point to by review season, not just a year of hard work nobody noticed.

Step 3 · Build your case

Write your wins the way a committee reads them.

When review season arrives, nobody remembers what they shipped back in March. Your coach helps you log your wins as they happen and write them up in terms of impact, scope, and results, rather than a flat list of tasks you completed.

You walk into the review with a packet that makes the decision an easy one.

Step 4 · Your manager

Make your manager your advocate.

Your manager usually argues your case in a room you're not in. Work Coach's recorder sits in on your 1:1s. No bot joins the call, and nothing leaves your Mac. Your coach helps you use that time to set the expectation early, share your progress, and ask directly what "ready" looks like.

By review season, your manager is repeating your case, not hearing it for the first time.

Frequently asked questions

Will my manager know Work Coach is in our 1:1?

No. Work Coach captures audio locally on your Mac. There is no bot in the call and no notification, so nobody else in the meeting can tell it's there.

What do I need to get started?

Start your free trial and tell your coach about your current level and the one you're targeting. It benchmarks you, builds a plan, and takes it from there. Every plan begins with a 2-week free trial, with no credit card required.

My company doesn't have a clear leveling rubric. Does this still work?

Yes. Your coach works from what the next level looks like in practice at companies like yours, and from what your own manager says in your 1:1s. You can also share any internal rubric you do have, and it'll calibrate to that.

How far ahead should I start?

The earlier the better. The strongest cases are built over a couple of quarters, not the week before review. But even if review season is close, your coach helps you assemble the strongest case from the work you've already done.

How does the rehearsal work?

Your coach plays your manager and pushes back the way they might, including a "not this cycle." You make your case out loud, get feedback, and run it again until you can handle the objections without losing your footing.

Is my data private?

Yes. Your recordings, transcripts, brag doc, and notes stay private to you. They're never shared with your employer or other users, and never used to train shared models. You can delete everything anytime.

What does it cost?

Plans start at $100/month for the AI coach, $250/month to add human review, and $500/month for live coaching sessions. A single promotion can be worth far more than a year of coaching. Every plan starts with a 2-week free trial. See pricing.
