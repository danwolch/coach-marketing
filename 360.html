The 12-person lineup that makes a 360 honest

We recommend at least 12 voices: 4 above you, 4 peers, 4 reports. Anything less creates echo chambers.

Leadership (4)

Manager, skip-level, and two senior leaders who depend on your work.

Peers (4)

Cross-functional partners who feel the impact of your collaboration daily.

Reports (4)

Direct reports or ICs downstream from your decisions—the candor you rarely hear.

You can add more voices, but this mix gives you the full org-level mirror without overwhelming anyone.

Why it works better than a perf review

  • More depth than manager-only reviews; you hear how peers really experience you.
  • Anonymity toggles let you separate sharp truth from identity to keep relationships healthy.
  • Coach knows your org, products, and politics—no generic leadership templates.
  • Follow-ups include scripts so you can close loops and build trust fast.

Charlie Munger’s inversion: how to sabotage a 360 (and how we prevent it)

If you wanted the 360 to fail…

  • Ask only the people who already praise you and ignore dissenting peers.
  • Collect vague platitudes instead of hard examples—so nothing can change.
  • Make it unsafe to share; attach names to every sharp comment.
  • Delay synthesis until the annual review so feedback is stale.
  • Hide the action plan so no one knows you listened.

Work Coach flips the script

  • We recruit a balanced 4/4/4 panel and weight feedback by proximity to outcomes.
  • Interviews use pointed prompts to pull specific examples, not generic praise.
  • Anon where it matters; sources stay protected so candor increases.
  • Insights synthesized in 24 hours with trend tags and priority calls.
  • Ready-to-send follow-ups show stakeholders you acted on what they shared.

What happens after the cycle

Your coach doesn’t disappear. We keep you accountable to the actions that earn trust back.

  • Weekly 20-minute voice sessions to review progress against the action plan.
  • Receipts you can bring to your manager to show growth before the next review.
  • Role-play sensitive conversations so you close gaps without damaging relationships.
  • Momentum tracking with reminders tailored to your org’s rhythms.

Signals we monitor

  • Influence on decisions and roadmap changes.
  • Reliability and follow-through as seen by peers.
  • Leadership presence: how often you proactively communicate.
  • Health of your direct reports: clarity, morale, and trust.
