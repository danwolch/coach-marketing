Step 1 · Your 30-60-90 plan

Replace "just figure it out" with a real plan.

Tell your coach about the role and the team you're joining, and it helps you build a 30-60-90 plan: what to learn first, who to meet, and what to deliver by each milestone. It's built around your level and the specific role you're stepping into.

So you show up with a plan, rather than waiting around to be told what to do.

  • By 60

  • By 90

Every team has an org chart and a real map that looks nothing like it. Your coach helps you figure out who actually makes decisions, who to build trust with early, and the unwritten rules no one puts in the onboarding doc.

Most people only learn this after stepping on a landmine or two. You get a head start.

Step 3 · First impressions

Make your first weeks count.

Your intro message, your first standup, the first time you speak up: these are the moments people use to size you up. Your coach helps you get the wording right so you sound confident and curious without looking like you're trying too hard.

You can rehearse the ones that matter until they feel natural.

Frequently asked questions

Will my new coworkers know Work Coach is there?

No. Work Coach captures audio locally on your Mac. There is no bot in the call and no notification, so nobody else in the meeting can tell it's there.

What do I need to get started?

Start your free trial and tell your coach about the role you're starting and the team you're joining. It helps you build a 30-60-90 plan and takes it from there. Every plan begins with a 2-week free trial, with no credit card required.

I haven't started yet. Is it too early?

It's the perfect time. The best first 90 days are planned before day one. Your coach helps you walk in with a plan, questions ready, and a clear idea of how you want to show up.

Does this work for a new manager role too?

Yes. The plan and people-mapping apply to any new role. If you're stepping into management, take a look at our page for managers, which goes deeper on 1:1s, feedback, and developing your team.

How does the rehearsal work?

Your coach plays the other person in the room, say your new manager or a teammate, and reacts the way they might. You practice out loud, get feedback, and run it again until the real thing feels familiar.

Is my data private?

Yes. Your recordings, transcripts, and notes stay private to you. They're never shared with your employer or other users, and never used to train shared models. You can delete everything anytime.

What does it cost?

Plans start at $100/month for the AI coach, $250/month to add human review, and $500/month for live coaching sessions. Every plan starts with a 2-week free trial. See pricing.
