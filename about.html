About Work Coach

Why I named the coach Jane

A tribute to my mom, my greatest coach

My mom was my first and most important coach. She didn't have the title, but she had the instinct—the ability to see what I couldn't see in myself and help me find my way forward.

She knew how to ask the right questions. How to challenge me without crushing me. How to celebrate wins while keeping me grounded. That's what great coaches do—they don't just tell you what to do, they help you figure out who you want to become.

Since her passing, I've felt the absence of that coaching presence. Someone who knew my context, understood my goals, and helped me think through the decisions that mattered.

I named this coach Jane because I wanted to honor what she gave me. A voice in my corner. Someone who believes in me and pushes me to be better.

"Everyone deserves someone in their corner who helps them see clearly, think deeply, and move forward with confidence."
In memory of Jane

About me

Building products and helping others grow

I'm Dan Wolchonok, currently VP of New Products at Reforge, the premier professional development platform backed by Andreessen Horowitz and used by teams at Google, Netflix, Airbnb, and hundreds of other companies.

My career has been built around understanding what helps people grow. I founded PrepWork (acquired by HubSpot in 2013), spent five years at HubSpot leading product and analytics teams, and have spent the last six years at Reforge building the data infrastructure and new products that help thousands of professionals level up.

At HubSpot, I helped grow Sidekick from 1,000 to over 300,000 weekly active users. My colleagues called me "Data Dan" because I measured everything—not for the sake of metrics, but to understand what actually worked. I learned that the best growth comes from deeply understanding people—what they need, where they get stuck, and how to help them succeed.

That same philosophy drives Work Coach. This isn't about AI replacing human connection—it's about making coaching accessible to everyone who wants to invest in themselves but doesn't have access to a $25,000 executive coach.

I built Work Coach because I believe everyone deserves what my mom gave me: someone who helps you think clearly, see your blind spots, and move forward with confidence.
