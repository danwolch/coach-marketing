Meet the founder

Dan Wolchonok

20 years building products and growth systems at high-growth technology companies.

Background

Dan's career spans founding an acquired startup, five years at HubSpot on the founding team of HubSpot Sales, and seven years at Reforge helping scale the company from five employees to 180.

After earning a Computer Science degree from Tufts University, Dan spent three years in management consulting at PwC, followed by three years at Microsoft as a Technical Lead working on enterprise search systems.

In 2011, Dan enrolled at Yale School of Management. During his MBA, he founded PrepWork—a personal research assistant that was acquired by HubSpot in 2013, just nine months after founding.

That acquisition launched a five-year career at HubSpot, where Dan rose to Director of Product Analytics and was on the founding team of HubSpot Sales—now over $1 billion in ARR.

Why the coach is named Jane

"I named the coach Jane after my mom. She was the person I'd go to with questions and look for advice. It was invaluable to get her perspective."
Dan Wolchonok

About Jane

Jane Wolchonok built a distinguished career in banking over four decades, rising from entry-level customer service to Senior Vice President at Brookline Bank. Her journey is the kind of growth that everyone deserves support for.

Jane started at Arm and Hammer answering customer questions. Strong female mentors there inspired her to pursue an MBA at NYU Stern—a credential that transformed her career trajectory.

That MBA launched a banking career spanning Citibank, Citizens Bank, HarborOne Bank, and ultimately Brookline Bank, where she served as SVP of Community Banking. She led strategic branch expansions and was quoted in press releases as a company spokesperson.

Throughout Dan's career, Jane was his sounding board—the person who knew his context, asked the right questions, and gave honest perspective. Work Coach is built to give everyone that same advantage.
